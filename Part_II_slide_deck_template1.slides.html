Prosper Loan Data Analysis

by Mohammed Barakah

Investigation Overview

In this analysis, my primary objective is to identify the most influential variables for predicting Borrower APR. I will primarily concentrate on BorrowerAPR, Loan amount, Prosper Score Amount, and the loan term.

Dataset Overview

This dataset originates from Prosper, the pioneering peer-to-peer lending platform in the United States, known for facilitating over $7 billion in funded loans. It comprises 113,937 individual loans, each associated with 81 distinct variables.

Borrower Rate distribution

The BorrowerAPR within the dataset spans a range of values, starting at approximately 6% as the lowest and reaching about 43% as the highest. The distribution of the Borrower Annual Percentage Rate displays a multimodal pattern, indicating the presence of three or more peaks, with the highest peak occurring around 36%.

What is relationship of ProsperScore Rating and Loan Amount on BorrowerAPR

Borrowers with higher score ratings tend to enjoy more favorable BorrowerAPRs, regardless of the loan amount they take. Conversely, borrowers with lower score ratings tend to face higher BorrowerAPRs, regardless of the loan amount they borrow.

Does the term have a significance effect on the loan and APR

The trend indicates that loans with a longer term (5 years) tend to have higher borrower APRs. This implies that as individuals collect loans over an extended period, their APRs increase. This aligns with the notion that longer-term loans involve greater risk, which could explain the higher APRs.
